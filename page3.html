EcoQuest



Design Thinking for agile innovation

At Metropolia University in Finland, I engaged in a course where we explored the Design Thinking process for agile innovation. The goal was to design a gamified tool that helps people to behave more sustainable all while diving into the phases of the design thinking microcycle.

As an outcome of this class, we developed ECOQuest. An app, that helps people to stay sustainable.

Design Thinking Microcycle

Design thinking is a problem-solving methodology that emphasizes empathy, ideation, and prototyping to address complex challenges. The microcycle, in this context, represents the smaller, repetitive stages within the design thinking framework. These stages typically include:



01 Understand

"Design a gamified tool that helps people to behave more sustainable"

Breaking down the question, we came to the following building blocks: gamified Tool target group sustainable

02 Observe

After breaking down the main question and determining interview topics, we entered the observation phase.

This involved interviewing individuals, such as friends and family, to gather their opinions on living sustainably. The questions were tailored to explore personal perspectives on sustainability.

03 POV and 04 Generate Ideas

Persona

To focus on our target group and help with the prototype, we made a persona.
It represents the main users and ensures that we will cope with their exact needs when using the app. Creating Shante Quinn`s persona wasn't just about the design; it`s about making sure our tool fits easily into her life, making things better for her and to be more sustainable.

05 Prototype

“EMPOWER CHANGE, ONE QUEST AT A TIME”

Introducing "Eco Quest" an innovative app designed to bridge the gap between intention and action when it comes to sustainability. Our app addresses the common problem of individuals wanting to make a positive impact on the environment but lacking a clear roadmap and motivation to do so consistently.

Create an account

Eco Quest empowers users to take meaningful steps toward sustainability through a gamified experience that is both engaging and impactful.

Users can choose from a diverse range of quests and characters, each targeting a specific area of sustainable living. These quests are designed to fit seamlessly into users' daily routines, making eco-friendly choices more accessible.

Track your sustainability

EcoQuest ensures that the user is able to check various aspects of their sustainability journey, including viewing their own profile as well as their peers. They can keep an eye
on their daily progress, track targets that are yet to be achieved, and monitor the aggregate amount of CO2 they have saved up to that point.

See your friend's score

See your friends accomplishments, a comprehensive overview of their daily score, observe the targets they have yet to accomplish, and review the aggregate amount of CO2 they have saved up to that specific point in time. This promotes a sense of accountability bur also promotes a shared commitment to sustainable goals.

Manage your settings

Users can perform several actions related to their account settings. This includes the option to edit their account details, tailor privacy settings to their preference, review and manage agreements, and customize the user interface settings according to their liking.

Conclusion

In this course, I learned how Design Thinking is a crucial part of engineering and how it can be applied to user experience. I collaborated with other international students at Metropolia University to create a concept that enhanced my understanding of the requirements in each step of the design cycle. Working together with a team of individuals with diverse backgrounds and perspectives was a unique aspect of the course. This collaborative environment not only enhanced creativity but also provided the richness that diverse viewpoints bring to the design process.

Making the course an invaluable experience in my academic and professional development.
