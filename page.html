Quartier G - Alte Gießerei

What is Quartier G - alte Gießerei?

design project second semester UXD

The Quartier G - alte Gießerei is a design project which was undertaken in the second semester of my User Experience Design study program.

It is an area in Ingolstadt that encompasses seven institutes and companies. Our task was to aesthetically enhance this area through design elements. This involved creating a logo, icons, site map, wayfinding system, data visualization, and interface design.

Logo

Icons

When creating icons, I tried to make them as similiar as possible to my existing logo. Icons play a crucial role in navigation and should speak a consistent visual language. They contribute to enhancing the user experience and convey essential information at a glance.

Location Plan

A site plan is intended to provide a comprehensive overview of the Quartier G. To achieve this, I highlighted the entire area with a distinctive frame. This clear demarcation should lead to easy orientation and provide viewers with an immediate understanding of the entire location of Quartier G.

Wayfinding System

Another challenge was to develop a comprehensive wayfinding system that enhances navigation in outdoor areas, buildings, and across multiple floors.
My goal was to present not only functional but also visually appealing solutions that enable a smooth and pleasant navigation experience in various environments. Click on the arrows to see my different solutions!

Data Visualization

Data visualization was also a focal point in our project. For this aspect, I chose to visualize the history of the Quartier G. Using illustrations and a timeline, to showcase the backstory to the different insitutions.

Moreover, my poster is currently on display at an exhibition in the Ingolstadt City Museum.

Interface Designs

Mobile App Quartier G


For the first interface, I chose to design an app to provide users with information about individual companies and institutes in the area, along with a navigation system to different sections. Considering the diverse target audience, the emphasis here was on an intuitive and user-friendly design.

Website Quartier G


A website was chosen as another interface to provide information for the area. Here, users can access information about individual institutes as well.
A gallery featuring images from the area and a link to the app are typical elements found on the website.
